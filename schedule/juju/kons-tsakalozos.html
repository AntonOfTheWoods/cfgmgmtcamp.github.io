#cfgmgmtcamp

6 and 7 February 2017

Gent, Belgium

Sharing Operational Knowledge and Secrets

Tuesday, February 7, 14:00 - B4.039

Juju is an open source application modelling and orchestration approach by Canonical. With Juju, users can deploy and maintain their infrastructure in a rich variety of target environments that include almost any cloud, local machines and bare metal systems. To produce an application package (termed charm) for Juju, you need to encapsulate the respective operational logic in charms. Using layers for charm authoring introduces you to a highly collaborative environment for sharing operational "secrets" and best practices. In this talk, we demonstrate how Juju promotes knowledge sharing. We show the layers that make up the Apache Spark charm, namely Basic, Apt, Puppet, Bigtop and Spark. Out of those five layers, three (Basic, Apt, Puppet) were consumed as third party offerings! Only the last layer (Spark) is Spark specific, while the Bigtop layer holds configuration "magic" among all Apache Bigtop derived charms.

  • Konstatinos Tsakalozos is currently employed by Canonical Ltd on the Big Data group of Juju. Prior to that he worked on Big Data for Microsoft. In 2012 he got his Ph.D. from the Department of Informatics at the University of Athens. His interests include cloud computing, distributed architectures and multidimensional indexing.
